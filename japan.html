How to Get There

To get to Japan from Manila, follow these steps:

  • Book a Flight: Book a direct flight from Manila (Ninoy Aquino International Airport) to Japan. The main international airports in Japan are Tokyo Narita (NRT), Tokyo Haneda (HND), Osaka Kansai (KIX), and Fukuoka (FUK). Direct flights from Manila to Tokyo usually take around 4-5 hours. If you're flying to other cities like Osaka, the flight duration is similar.
  • Flight Options: Choose airlines like Philippine Airlines (PAL), Japan Airlines (JAL), All Nippon Airways (ANA), Cebu Pacific, or AirAsia. These airlines offer direct flights to major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto.
  • Arrival in Japan: Upon arrival at any of Japan's international airports, you will go through immigration and customs. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your stay, and be prepared to show proof of your return ticket and accommodation details.
  • Transportation from the Airport: After clearing customs, you can take taxis, trains, buses, or ride-sharing services to your destination within the city or across Japan. Japan's public transportation system is highly efficient, with a wide network of trains, subways, and buses that make it easy to travel between cities.
  • Visa Requirements: Philippine passport holders need a tourist visa to enter Japan. You will need to apply for a visa through the Japan Embassy or Consulate in the Philippines. Required documents include a valid passport, completed visa application form, proof of accommodation, return ticket, and financial statements to prove sufficient funds for your stay.

Places to Visit in Japan

Tokyo

Tokyo is Japan's vibrant and bustling capital, known for its unique blend of modernity and tradition. It's a city of contrasts, where futuristic skyscrapers stand alongside ancient temples and shrines. Tokyo offers something for everyone: from the high-energy streets of Shibuya and Shinjuku, to the tranquil Meiji Shrine and serene parks. Famous for its cutting-edge technology, shopping districts like Harajuku and Ginza, and rich culture, Tokyo is a must-visit for anyone wanting to experience Japan’s dynamic lifestyle, world-class food, and iconic landmarks.

Kyoto

Kyoto, once the imperial capital of Japan, is a city steeped in history and tradition. Famous for its well-preserved temples, shrines, and beautiful gardens, Kyoto offers a glimpse into Japan's past. Visitors can explore iconic sites like the Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), and the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove. Kyoto’s charming tea houses, traditional ryokan inns, and vibrant geisha culture in districts like Gion provide a unique and serene atmosphere, making it a perfect destination for those seeking cultural immersion and natural beauty.

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, Japan's highest peak at 3,776 meters, is an iconic symbol of the country. Located on Honshu Island, it is a dormant stratovolcano renowned for its symmetrical, snow-capped beauty. Mount Fuji attracts hikers, nature lovers, and photographers alike, with the climbing season offering an opportunity to ascend its slopes. Surrounding the mountain are stunning lakes, hot springs, and scenic views, making it a UNESCO World Heritage site. As a revered spiritual symbol, it also appears frequently in traditional Japanese art and culture, embodying both natural majesty and cultural significance.

Osaka

Osaka, Japan's third-largest city, is known for its vibrant atmosphere, rich history, and modern attractions. Famous for its delicious food, Osaka is the birthplace of dishes like takoyaki (octopus balls) and okonomiyaki (savory pancakes). The city blends tradition with innovation, offering landmarks like the historic Osaka Castle and bustling districts such as Dotonbori, filled with neon lights and entertainment.
