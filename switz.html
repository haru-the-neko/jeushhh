How to Get There

To get to Switzerland from Manila, follow these steps:

  • Book a Flight: Book a direct or connecting flight from Manila (Ninoy Aquino International Airport) to Switzerland. The main international airports in Switzerland are Zurich Airport (ZRH), Geneva Airport (GVA), and Basel-Mulhouse Airport (BSL). Direct flights are not typically available from Manila, so you will most likely have to take connecting flights with layovers in major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, Istanbul, or Bangkok. Flight duration for connecting flights can range from 15-20 hours, depending on layovers.
  • Flight Options: Choose airlines like Emirates, Qatar Airways, Turkish Airlines, or Cathay Pacific. These airlines offer multiple routes and flight schedules with layovers in their respective hubs. Alternatively, you can also fly with European carriers like Lufthansa, Swiss International Air Lines, or Air France with layovers in Europe.
  • Arrival in Switzerland: Once you arrive in Switzerland, you will go through immigration and customs at the airport (Zurich, Geneva, Basel, etc.). Ensure that your passport and visa requirements are up to date.
  • Transportation from the Airport: After clearing customs, you can take taxis, ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft), or public transportation to your destination within the city or across Switzerland. Switzerland has an excellent public transport system, including trains, buses, and trams, which makes it easy to travel to various cities and towns.
  • Visa Requirements: Philippine passport holders require a valid Schengen visa to enter Switzerland, as Switzerland is part of the Schengen Area. Ensure that you apply for a Schengen visa before your trip. You will need to provide proof of accommodation, return flight bookings, and travel insurance for the duration of your stay.

Places to Visit in Switzerland

Zermatt and the Matterhorn

Zermatt, a car-free village in the Swiss Alps, is the gateway to the iconic Matterhorn mountain. Known for its charming alpine atmosphere, it offers skiing in winter and hiking in summer. Visitors can enjoy stunning views from the Gornergrat Railway and Matterhorn Glacier Paradise. Zermatt is also a popular spot for mountaineers aiming to conquer the Matterhorn, providing a year-round alpine adventure.

Lucerne

Lucerne is a picturesque city in central Switzerland, known for its well-preserved medieval architecture, stunning lake views, and surrounding mountains. Highlights include the iconic Chapel Bridge, the Lion Monument, and easy access to Mount Pilatus and Mount Rigi. Lucerne blends natural beauty with cultural charm, making it a top destination for travelers.

Interlaken

Interlaken is a scenic Swiss town nestled between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, surrounded by the iconic peaks of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains. Known for its stunning natural beauty, it offers a wide range of outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and paragliding. With its charming streets, traditional Swiss chalets, and vibrant cultural scene, Interlaken attracts both adventure seekers and those looking to relax, making it a top destination in Switzerland.

Jungfraujoch

Jungfraujoch, often referred to as the "Top of Europe," is a famous mountain pass in the Bernese Alps, standing at 3,454 meters (11,332 feet). It is home to the highest railway station in Europe, which is accessible via the Jungfrau Railway. The summit offers breathtaking views of surrounding glaciers, including the Aletsch Glacier, and stunning alpine peaks like the Eiger and Mönch mountains. At the top, visitors can explore an ice palace, walk on observation decks, and enjoy various snow-related activities. Jungfraujoch is a must-visit destination for those seeking natural beauty and adventure in Switzerland.
